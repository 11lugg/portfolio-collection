Background

Port Police

I began my working life doing part time jobs during my full time college studies. My first career move however was working in the port of Bristol Police. I had always wanted to be a police officer as a child. I wanted to run around shooting bad guys.

Paralegal

During my time in the police I wanted learn more about how laws are created and how they evolve and I began a law degree with the open university. During this time I wanted to get a real world understanding of the industry and began working as a paralegal in a conveyancing team. I realised that law wasn't right for me, purely because of the competition.

TV Extra on BBC period drama Wolf Hall

Very randomly and during my employment as a paralegal, I was walking down the street and was stopped by a casting agent. She said I looked perfect for a role they were casting extras for and gave me her business card. Naturally, I had to find out more and was cast in Wolf Hall for 3 months. I was incredibly lucky and met people from all different backgrounds. I played a main servant of Henry VIII which meant that whenever he was on the scene, you will find me lurking behind.

embarrasing picture alert!!

Travelling Australia and working in sales

I had always planned to go to Australia and following Wolf Hall, I thought there was no better time. During my time travelling I lived and worked in Sydney, Melbourne and Broome (WA). To support myself, I got into commission only sales jobs, selling ADT alarm systems and charities on the streets. I loved every minute of it; the sights, the climate and the people were great. I also saw alot of similarities between studying law and sales.

Universal outdoor

All good things come to an end. I loved Australia but I missed my family and friends. It was time to come home. Once I returned, I wanted to continue in sales and learn more. The first opportunity came in the form of Universal outdoor. I was selling advertising space to businesses in shopping centres, cinema and golf clubs.

Ordoo

Ordoo is an app to order and pay in advance at your favourite cafes, coffee shops, lunch spots and bars. Great if you don't have the time to stand in a queue

Unfortuntely, 7 months after I started, Universal Outdoor went into administration and I was out of work. At the time, I had moved from Weston-super-Mare to Bath and stumbled on the founder of an app called Ordable (later renamed Ordoo). He had developed an MVP and was trialling it at a coffee shop; which I just happened to be at. I introduced myself and thought the concept sounded exciting and I started and headed up the business development and account management efforts. This has to have been the best hands on experience I have ever had. I had the freedom to create and iterate on processes, I had a say in the product development, I pitched on a quarterly basis to the board and I pitched for investment. The commercial accumen and the market knowledge I gained was invalueable.

Contracting for startups

As much as I love Ordoo, being part of a startup that hasn't found product market fit (yet) means is reliant on ongoing investment until it does. I couldn't personally afford to stay. I still hold my shares in Ordoo however, and I still advise the team. The startup environment is brilliant and being part of that community allows you to get recognised. I was being approached by a number of startups needing help with their sales and account management efforts. How could I refuse?!

District sales manager for Quandoo

I was headhunted by a growth stage food tech company called Quandoo, looking to expand its footprint in the South West. This company had found product market fit and I saw this as a great opportunity in taking my skills to the next level.
